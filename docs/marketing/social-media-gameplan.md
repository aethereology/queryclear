# Social Media Gameplan — queryclear

> A solo-operator playbook. The goal is not "be on social media." The goal is a
> **founder-led proof engine** that (1) drives free audits, (2) builds credibility,
> and (3) doubles as GEO fuel — so queryclear itself shows up when someone asks an
> AI engine "who does AI search optimization." Paste-ready copy + templates below.
>
> Brand voice (non-negotiable): plain, concrete, honest. No hype buzzwords. We sell
> **readiness, not guarantees** — never promise rankings or AI citations.

---

## TL;DR

1. **One spine, not five channels:** founder-led on **LinkedIn + X**, with a `queryclear`
   brand account that mostly amplifies. Add **one** vertical channel (Instagram for a
   chosen vertical, e.g. med spas) only after the rhythm is automatic.
2. **One flagship format:** the **AI Visibility Teardown** — run the free audit on a real
   (or anonymized) business, show what AI engines say vs. should. It *is* the product, demonstrated.
3. **One CTA, everywhere:** *Run the free audit* → `queryclear.com/free-audit`.
4. **One flywheel:** every audit you do becomes 3–4 posts. You publish what you already do.
5. **Cadence:** 2–3 posts/week, 1 teardown/week, batched in one sitting. Consistency > volume.

## 1. Account architecture

- **`queryclear` brand account = ~30%.** Exists mainly to be a *consistent, citeable entity*
  (clean name/bio/links = GEO hygiene) and to amplify the founder. Don't expect it to perform alone.
- **Pick ONE primary platform to start** (LinkedIn *or* X). Cross-post to the other; don't
  treat them as two workloads.

## 4. The flagship format: the AI Visibility Teardown

This is the engine. It's your product, shown working, in public. Make it repeatable.

### Ethics / permission (read first)
- **Real, named business:** get written permission, or only show *publicly observable* engine
  output (what ChatGPT says when asked) and keep your fixes constructive — never punch down.
- **No permission / sensitive:** anonymize. "A med spa in a mid-size metro" + blurred specifics.
- **Never** fabricate engine output or results. Screenshot real prompts. If a result is a
  modeled estimate, say so. This is the whole brand — protect it.

### LinkedIn carousel / X thread skeleton (7 beats)

1. **Hook (the gap):** "I asked ChatGPT for the best [service] in [city]. It named 3 businesses.
   [Business] — who's genuinely good — wasn't one of them."
2. **Why it matters:** "That's the new front page. If the AI doesn't know you exist, the customer never does."
3. **What the machine sees:** screenshot / quote of how the engine describes (or ignores) them.
   "Here's what it 'knows' about them: [thin / wrong / nothing]."
4. **The diagnosis (3 findings):** "Three reasons: ① no Organization/LocalBusiness schema ②
   service pages don't say what/who/where plainly ③ no content answering the actual buyer question."
5. **The fix (concrete):** "What I'd do first: [specific, plain-English step]." Show the before/after of one thing.
6. **The honest line:** "This doesn't guarantee citations — nothing does. It makes the site
   genuinely worth citing. That's the part you control."
7. **CTA:** "Want to see how AI describes *your* business? The audit is free and instant: queryclear.com/free-audit"

### 60-second video / Short script

> [On screen: a real ChatGPT prompt] "Watch — I'll ask AI to recommend a [service] near me…
> [results] Notice who's missing. Here's why: their site has no schema, the service page is
> vague, and there's nothing answering the question a buyer actually asks. Fixing that won't
> *guarantee* anything — but right now they're invisible, and that's fixable. Run a free audit
> on your own site, link's below."

### Formatting for the algorithm AND for AI engines (GEO)
- Put the core claim in the **first line** (LinkedIn truncates; engines weight openings).
- Use real entity names and locations where permitted (citeable specifics).
- Add 2–3 plain hashtags max (#AISearch #SEO #GEO). Don't keyword-stuff.
- Cross-post the same teardown as a short LinkedIn article occasionally — long-form pages get
  indexed and cited more than ephemeral posts.

## 5. Repurposing flywheel — one audit → 4 posts (checklist)

Every time you run an audit (free-tool or paid), spin it into content. Copy this checklist:

```
[ ] 1. TEARDOWN post (carousel/thread) — the 7-beat format above. Pillar 1.
[ ] 2. CLIP (30–60s) — screen-record the engine prompt + your top finding. Pillar 1. (Shorts/Reels)
[ ] 3. EDUCATION post — pull ONE finding into a standalone "here's what X is and why it matters". Pillar 4.
[ ] 4. POV/PROOF note — a short observation it sparked ("3rd dentist this week with no schema…"). Pillar 2/3.
[ ] 5. (Optional) Add the anonymized finding to a running "AI visibility log" you post monthly.
[ ] CTA on every one: queryclear.com/free-audit
[ ] Permission check done (named) OR anonymized (sensitive).
```

You are not "making content." You are **publishing the work you already do.** That's what makes
this survivable for one person.

## 7. Cadence & batching (the solo workflow)

- **Batch once a week** (~90 min): run 1 audit → produce the teardown + clip + education post →
  schedule all three. Don't touch socials the other days except to reply.
- **Reply > post.** 10 min/day answering comments and DMs beats a 4th post. Conversations convert.
- **Schedule** with anything (LinkedIn native scheduler, Buffer free tier). Removing the daily
  "what do I post" decision is what keeps this alive.
- **Don't add a platform** until the current one is automatic and boring. Boring = working.

---

## 8. Honesty guardrails (same as the site — these protect the whole brand)

- No guaranteed-ranking or guaranteed-citation language. Ever.
- No fabricated engine output, screenshots, or results. Real prompts only; mark modeled estimates as estimates.
- Anonymize any client-identifying detail without **written** permission.
- No fake testimonials, follower-buying, or engagement pods. Your edge is being the credible one
  in a hype-soaked category — don't trade it for a vanity spike.
- Punch up at the category's snake-oil, never down at a specific business you're teaching about.

## 9. Metrics that matter (and the ones to ignore)

**Track (weekly):**
- Free audits run / week (the real funnel metric — does social drive `/free-audit`?).
- Profile views + connection/follow requests from your target audience.
- DMs and replies that turn into conversations.
- Saves + shares on teardowns (intent signal > likes).
- Branded search / "queryclear" mentions appearing in AI answers (check monthly: ask
  ChatGPT/Perplexity "who does AI search optimization" and see if you surface — your own GEO scorecard).

**Ignore:** raw follower count, likes, reach. They feel good and pay nothing.

> Rule: if a content experiment doesn't move "free audits run" within ~3–4 weeks, change the
> content, not the cadence.

## 10. GEO hygiene for your own profiles (practice what you sell)

You sell entity clarity and machine-legibility — your own footprint must model it:

- **One consistent name** ("queryclear") and one-line description across every platform, your
  site's Organization schema, and your `llms.txt`. Engines reconcile entities across sources.
- **Link every profile to `queryclear.com`** (and the free audit). Inbound consistency = stronger entity.
- **Publish at least some long-form** (LinkedIn articles / a simple blog). Durable URLs get cited;
  ephemeral posts mostly don't.
- **Make claims specific and structured** (named services, locations, plain definitions) — the same
  thing you tell clients. Your social corpus becomes part of what AI engines read about you.
- The win condition: when someone asks an AI engine "who helps businesses show up in AI search,"
  **queryclear is in the answer.** That's the strategy proving itself.
