## 1) Troniex Technologies — the real prospect

### The honest finding (all verified 2026-06-19)
- Their **machine layer is ahead of most**: Organization schema, a clean `llms.txt`, and a
  robots.txt that explicitly welcomes `GPTBot` / `ChatGPT-User` / `OAI-SearchBot`. Lead with
  this — it's a genuine compliment and it's true.
- The gap is the **visible answer layer**:
  - **No FAQ and no `FAQPage` schema anywhere.** Their buyers ask very specific questions —
    "how much to build a crypto exchange?", "how long does ERC-20 token development take?",
    "white-label vs custom exchange?" — and the site answers none of them in a way an engine
    can lift.
  - **Every heading is a slogan, not a fact.** H1 = *"Digital Transformation Isn't a Choice
    It's a Strategy!"*; H2s = *"From Vision to Victory!"*, *"Turn Ambition into Architecture"*,
    *"Rewrite the Rules of Your Industry."* When an engine summarizes the page, it has marketing
    copy where it wants *"Troniex builds crypto exchanges, DeFi platforms, and AI agents."*
  - Net: their schema/llms.txt say the right thing, but the **content a human and an engine
    actually read is all slogan.** The two don't reinforce each other.

> ⚠️ Do NOT claim "ChatGPT doesn't mention you" unless you actually run the prompt and confirm
> it. If you want that hook, run *"Best blockchain / crypto exchange development companies?"* in
> ChatGPT + Perplexity first and screenshot the result. Otherwise lead with the on-page finding,
> which is 100% checkable.

### Caveats (be honest with yourself before sending)
- They are a **software/AI dev agency** — they may treat their own GEO as a DIY job. Lower close
  probability than a med spa.
- **Overseas (India), crypto/iGaming/casino vertical.** Fine as a prospect; just know it's
  outside your local lane and the vertical is sensitive. Decide if it's on-brand for you.

llms.txt (it lists "GEO / Generative Engine Optimization" as a topic), and multilingual sitemaps.

positioning. No contact needed.
