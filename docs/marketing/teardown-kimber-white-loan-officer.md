> Shmilovich. A Past President of the National Association of Mortgage Brokers — nowhere in
> the answer.

**Slide 5 — The diagnosis (3 findings)**
> Three reasons a résumé like that can stay invisible to AI:
>
> ① **No structured data.** His site has no schema — nothing that tells a machine "this is
> Kimber White, a licensed mortgage broker in Oakland Park / Fort Lauderdale." So it guesses.
>
> ② **The page opens with a slogan, not the facts.** The first thing a machine reads is
> *"Low Rates. No Hassle."* — not who he is, where he works, or who he helps.
>
> ③ **His authority is invisible.** 35 years, a NAMB presidency, a national award — none of it
> is stated in a way a machine can parse or connect back to NAMB. Humans see a legend.
> Machines see a generic page.

**Slide 6 — The fix (concrete, before/after)**
> What I'd do first: add Person + LocalBusiness schema, and rewrite the opening so it states
> who/what/where in plain language.
>
> **Before** (what the machine reads): "Low Rates. No Hassle."
>
> **After:** "Kimber White — licensed mortgage broker, NMLS #352949, in Oakland Park, FL,
> serving Fort Lauderdale and Broward County. 35+ years. Past President, NAMB."
>
> Same person. Now legible to a machine.
