## Reply When Someone Asks If This Guarantees AI Citations

No. Nobody credible can guarantee AI citations. AI answers vary by model, prompt, location, source set, and time. What we can control is whether the website is clearer, crawlable, structured, useful, and backed by proof.

## Reply When Someone Says "Isn't This Just SEO?"

Yes, mostly. That is the point. AI search is not a separate magic channel. It puts more pressure on the same fundamentals: crawlability, clarity, useful content, structure, trust signals, and local/service specificity.

## Reddit Comment Bank

Use these without links unless a subreddit clearly allows relevant resources.

1. I would start with homepage clarity before adding more content. Many local sites never plainly state category, service area, proof, and next step. That creates ambiguity for users and search systems.

2. The underrated issue is not "AI keywords." It is whether the page plainly says who the business is, what it does, where it serves, and why it should be trusted.

3. Schema is not a magic ranking switch. I think of it as ambiguity reduction. It helps machines confirm what the page already says: business type, service, location, FAQ, etc.

4. Broad service pages are often weaker than specific ones. "Services" or "injectables" gives search systems less to work with than pages that explain Botox, fillers, laser, provider credentials, city, FAQs, and next step.

5. No one controls whether an AI answer cites a business on a given prompt. The practical work is making the site easier to crawl, parse, verify, and summarize.
