## 2. Education post — "what 'schema' actually is" (Pillar 4)

> Pulls finding ① out of the teardown into a standalone. No business named — this one
> is evergreen and re-postable.

**Post:**

> Your website probably has no "schema." Here's what that means in plain English — and
> what it quietly costs you in AI search.
>
> Schema is a hidden label on your site that tells a machine what your business *is*:
> "This is [Name], a [licensed mortgage broker] in [Oakland Park, FL], serving [Fort
> Lauderdale]." It doesn't change what a human sees. It changes what a machine
> *understands*.
>
> No label → the AI has to guess from your page copy. And if your page opens with a
> slogan ("Low Rates. No Hassle.") instead of the facts, the guess is bad — or it
> skips you entirely and names a competitor who's easier to read.
>
> I watched this exact thing happen this week to one of the most decorated loan
> officers in the country. Elite reputation. Invisible to ChatGPT. The gap wasn't
> skill — it was a missing label.
>
> The fix is usually a 20-minute change: add the right schema, and lead the page with
> who/what/where in plain language. It doesn't guarantee citations — nothing does. It
> makes your site legible to the machines deciding who gets recommended.
>
> Want to know if yours has it? The audit checks this, free and instant →
